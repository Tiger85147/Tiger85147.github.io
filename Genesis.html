Ara's Genesis




About three years back, after our son scored well in SAT, we started our exploratory journey to seek support for him. We knew that we didn’t have enough relevant knowledge to provide guidance to him.

Well, we took the easy way first – Google-search for experts in Global University Admissions! Second – reached out to our son’s school seniors and their parents. Made a shortlist and met the top guys in the business!

Counselor A: Conducts a mock SAT. Every single student, I repeat, every single student gets exactly the same ‘low’ score! Yes, it’s always 1250! Then, you are told that they will help you to get 1500 (so that you can get into best Universities) if you enroll the student with them, which will cost you several tens of thousands of rupees. If that is how you start, imagine how you will end up! (Counseling services cost had some more zeroes, btw!)

Counselor AS: Humble to a fault, brilliant pedigree. And then an incredibly high price is quoted for ‘support through the admissions process’. And as we knew by then, there are no guarantees!

Counselor E: “It’s okay to ‘hype-up’ achievements; Universities don’t verify what you write anyways…” This, after you pay INR 700,000!!

Counselor J: Impressive first meeting with the owner. The moment you enroll (because of that meeting), that is the last you see of him! You spend all your time after that following-up with his team. You do deadline tracking, you keep calling ‘your-personal-counselor’ who is never available, your essay is always accepted (with almost no qualitative inputs; you wonder why you needed help then!) … you keep following up till you are just minutes away from application submission!

Counselor P: Didn’t know the difference between Early Action and Restrictive Early Action!

Counselor U: The chief didn’t know full form of STEM! Do I write more on this?!

Common among almost all of these: 1. Will call you a zillion times till you make payments. Then it’s the other way around!! 2. Limited hour service. You get only X hours of their time! 3. If you call and they aren’t available; well, they are busy. If they call and you aren’t available, you have ‘lost those hours’ for good! 4. They have affiliations (read: Paid Partnerships! Commission agents, you know!) with certain Universities – and you are ‘pushed hard’ to apply to such ‘very good colleges’!

Having lost ‘serious money’, we became ‘Atmanirbhar’! Surprised ourselves with the quality of admissions we got for our son and for some of this close friends in our very first attempt. One in global top 12 Universities. Ten more in global top 35 Universities. Nine of these were in Computer Science Engineering.

That is how Ara was conceived.

And yes, your experience with Ara will be like ‘none of the above’! Guaranteed!
